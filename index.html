The main problems in learning

Neither school nor university teaches us how to study the material properly. We prepare for exams and learn the tickets. We practice solving monotonous problems in order to pass the test better, but often in the end this does not give us real knowledge. Cramming quickly wears off and is not helpful.

Conclusion: You also need to be able to learn, but for some reason not many people teach it. What to do about it?

Specific techniques and exercises help change the approach to learning, making it effective and engaging. These same techniques are applied to the training in the Practicum.

Teaching Techniques

Five Practices by Barbara Oakley

Two types of attention

Deep knowledge arises when you alternate between focused and scattered thinking.

Recall

Recall what you've learned-it will allow you to connect the scattered portions of your memory.

Interleaving

Learn several skills at the same time, they enrich each other.

Questions

As you listen to the instructor, come up with a good question that you really care about.

The illusion of competence

Repeat it to yourself, write it down, tell a friend: you only seem to own the new topic.

Barbara Oakley's Story.

As a child, Barbara was not good at math. She considered herself a complete humanitarian, and not the smartest. In the army she studied Russian in order to get a raise, and so successfully that she was promoted to commanding officer. But in order to be promoted, she had to pass math. And then Barbara came up with her own approach to the exact sciences. It turned out that if you are "bad at something," your hard-won knowledge is much deeper than that of those who are clear at a glance.
